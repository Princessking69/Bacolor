Discover the Heart of Bacolor

Rich in history, culture, and resilience — welcome to Bacolor, Pampanga.

Bacolor is a heritage-rich town located in Pampanga, Philippines. Despite the devastation caused by Mount Pinatubo's eruption, Bacolor rose with pride, showcasing its deeply rooted traditions, architectural landmarks, and vibrant Kapampangan culture.

History of Bacolor

Bacolor dates back to 1571, originally known as "Baculud" (meaning high ground). It served as the capital of Pampanga and even as the temporary capital of the Philippines during the British invasion in the 1700s. Bacolor has endured natural disasters, colonial transitions, and wars, all while preserving its deep historical and cultural legacy.

Major Historical Events

  • 1571 - Founding of Bacolor, originally called "Baculud"
  • 1762-1764 - Served as temporary capital during British occupation
  • 1897 - Revolutionary forces used Bacolor as headquarters
  • 1991 - Mount Pinatubo eruption buried much of the town
  • 1995-Present - Ongoing rehabilitation and cultural revival

Top Attractions

San Guillermo Parish Church

Half-buried by lahar from the Mount Pinatubo eruption, this church remains an active place of worship and a testament to faith. Visitors enter through what was once a window but now serves as the main entrance.

Don Honorio Ventura Technological State University

The first state university in Pampanga, offering quality education and preserving local academic traditions.

Heritage Houses

Several ancestral homes showcase the Spanish and Filipino architectural fusion that characterized Bacolor before the volcanic disaster.

Natural Attractions

Despite the devastation from Mount Pinatubo, Bacolor has transformed some lahar-covered areas into eco-tourism sites. These areas now serve as both memorial spaces and recreational zones for visitors.

Culture & Traditions

Bacolor is known for its grand fiestas, notably the La Naval Fiesta every November. The town celebrates through processions, music, dances, and cuisine — all reflecting Kapampangan creativity and warmth.

La Naval Fiesta

The La Naval Festival is a vibrant religious and cultural event. The streets come alive with parades, traditional performances, and decorated carriages bearing saints. It is both a spiritual and festive showcase of Bacolor's faith and artistry.

Kapampangan Cuisine

Bacolor is home to some of the most delicious Kapampangan dishes. Local specialties include:

  • Sisig - A sizzling dish made from parts of pig's head and liver
  • Tocino - Sweet cured pork
  • Bringhe - The Kapampangan version of paella
  • San Nicolas Cookies - Traditional oval-shaped cookies
